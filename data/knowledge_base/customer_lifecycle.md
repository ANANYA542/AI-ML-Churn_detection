# Customer Lifecycle Best Practices

## Onboarding (Months 1-3)
During the first three months, customers are highly sensitive to billing confusion and setup difficulties.
- Provide a clear, personalized "first bill" walkthrough via video or dedicated call.
- Ensure any installation or activation issues have been completely resolved by calling them 14 days after service start.

## Mid-Contract Engagement (Months 6-12)
Avoid only contacting the customer when there is a problem.
- Conduct a "service check-in" call around month 6 to ensure the customer is maximizing their current plan.
- If underutilizing, suggest a downgrade or alternative service that fits better. It hurts short-term revenue but drastically improves long-term retention.

## Renewal Window (Last 3 Months of Contract)
Customers on fixed contracts start looking for alternatives near the end of their term.
- Proactively communicate exactly what their post-contract rate will be.
- Offer early-renewal incentives, such as a localized streaming package or a 10% discount to lock in another 12 to 24 months.

## Out-of-Contract / Month-to-Month
Month-to-month users are highly volatile and can leave with zero penalty.
- Tempt them into a fixed contract with aggressive, targeted pricing discounts that they cannot get on a month-to-month basis.
- Highlight loyalty rewards available exclusively to contracted members.
