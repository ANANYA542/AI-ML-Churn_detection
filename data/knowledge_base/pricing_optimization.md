# Pricing and Contract Optimization Insights

## Handling High Monthly Charges
When a customer is paying above the segment average, they frequently question the value they receive.
- Conduct a personalized audit. If they are overpaying for unused Add-Ons (like unused extra lines, premium channels), proactively suggest removing them to lower the bill.
- Consider offering a flat $10/month loyalty credit for 12 months rather than changing their core plan.

## The Role of Electronic Checks
Customers using manual payment methods (like Electronic Checks or mailed checks) churn more often because the payment process is a monthly reminder of the cost.
- Offer a one-time $20 bill credit or a permanent 5% discount if they switch to Auto-Pay via Credit Card or direct Bank Transfer.
- Auto-Pay removes the psychological friction of parting with money each month.

## Managing Contract Flexibility
If a customer is already month-to-month and indicates they want to leave due to high upfront costs of other plans:
- Give them a prorated exit plan or an "easy pause" feature if they are traveling or temporarily relocating, rather than fully canceling service. 
- For fixed-contract users seeking out because of financial hardship, waive the early termination fee (ETF) if they agree to a much cheaper, bare-bones plan instead.
