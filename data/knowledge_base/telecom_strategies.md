# Telecom Retention Strategies

## Strategy 1: Data Plan Upgrades
If a customer frequently exceeds their data limits or experiences throttling, they are at a high risk of churn. 
Provide a targeted, low-cost upgrade to an unlimited or higher-tier data plan for the remainder of their contract. 
This removes friction, improves immediate satisfaction, and often prevents them from comparing competitor rates.

## Strategy 2: Service Bundling
Single-service users (e.g., only internet or only phone) churn at higher rates than multi-service users.
Offer a significant first-year discount to bundle a secondary service. Getting a customer to bundle internet with phone service increases stickiness and switching costs.

## Strategy 3: Proactive Hardware Upgrades
Customers with older devices or routers are more likely to experience technical issues and attribute them to the carrier's network.
Flag customers with hardware older than 3 years. Offer a complementary router upgrade or a high-value phone trade-in deal to boost perceived value and perceived network quality.

## Strategy 4: VIP Support
For high Monthly Charge users who report technical issues frequently, assign them to a premium support tier. 
Waive any fees for tech-support visits. A dedicated line or priority routing solves their problem quickly, showing respect for their high expenditure.
